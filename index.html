About

I work at the intersection of climate action, community engagement, and governance, with a focus on designing and implementing pathways that are both environmentally sustainable and socially equitable. My work spans research, policy, and field-based interventions, engaging with diverse stakeholders across geographies to translate ideas into actionable impact.

I am particularly interested in understanding how local contexts shape climate responses, and how institutions can better align with community realities. Through my work, I aim to contribute to building systems that are adaptive, inclusive, and grounded in lived experiences.

My approach is inherently interdisciplinary—bringing together insights from development practice, policy design, and systems thinking to address complex challenges at the interface of people and the planet.
